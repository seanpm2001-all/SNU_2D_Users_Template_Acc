SNU developer log Week 47

SNU developer log day 323

I produced a smaller update today with a lot of commit changes again. I am going through one section of the HTTP status list per day, tomorrow is going to be the biggest one in the official range (since 4.xx has a lot of status codes)

I also did some JavaScript work, documentation changes, and improvements to some features such as the SNU Clock. My work ethic seems to be back and stable, and I have everything balanced. I am REALLY hoping it doesn't fall apart in the next 3 days (example: stopping website development for at least 1 day)

I have been getting a lot done lately, as of yesterday I have already reached 100 commits in the 3.0.6 period, in just 4 days. I still have a lot to do, and my research level is still increasing. I will hopefully be able to make major improvements soon.

I didn't do much web development research today, but I am getting something ready for tomorrow. I also have lots of plans for features at the moment.

Sean Patrick Myrick on December 19th 2019

SNU developer log day 324

I had another good day with development. Today I worked heavily on the HTTP documentation center, with the entire range of 4.x.x protocols, 1 5.x.x protocol, and a massive update to its main page. Along with this, I began experimenting with XHHTML, and I did upgrades to documentation.

Development so far is going well, but the commit count compared to update quality is a little low.

Lately, I have been using the update log to store notes instead of an external txt file. This has raised the size of the update log, but it provides documentation for myself. I may start removing older entries, as each day produces sometimes over 2 kilobytes, this builds up overtime.

The notes have been used as a Todo list of features and improvements to the site. The sections I follow most are 2 separate "todo today" areas, where I still don't get to everything.

I am starting to lose interest in the development of the HTTP list. It is a useful resource, but it isn't a major feature of the site. I am going to try and get it over with, then move on to the next thing.

I eventually had to stop, as I was just really tired of updating the site today, and I took a break. There also wasn't too much else to add in.

Sean Patrick Myrick on December 20th 2019
