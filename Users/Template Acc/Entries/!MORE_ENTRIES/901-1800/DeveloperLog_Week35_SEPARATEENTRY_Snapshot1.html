SNU developer log Week 35

SNU developer log day 239

I improved the site a little bit today, but not as much as I wanted. I added new scriptstarters for CSS stylesheets, and JavaScript functionality. I also implemented some other features, such as SNU news, some new icons, and improvements to the speedrunner page.

I got a networking problem fixed today, it was just a loose cable. It wasn't a cause of website disruption, but now the internet is fast again, and doesn't randomly disconnect every minute.

Sean Patrick Myrick on September 26th 2019

SNU developer log day 240

I had a lot of time to develop today. I ran into issues after school ended, as my focus just dies after school ends, and it is really annoying. This isn't a problem on the weekends, and if it is, it is a lot more controllable.

Today, I did massive improvements to the speedrun engine section, writing several engines in C, Assembly, and Java. All programming languages are similar in their own ways, and it will take me some time to get the syntax down. There is still research to be done as well.

I also added in an improved CSS scriptstarter, with some forgotten changes from yesterday, a documentation update, with a little templating for the developer log. Today went decent, I wanted to do more, but my focus died out, and I ran low on time.

It is the weekend, and I have a huge bucketlist, that might spill into next week.

Sean Patrick Myrick on September 27th 2019

SNU developer log day 241

I had a bad day with development today. I was distracted by other projects, and took too much break time. Good thing is, I got a lot of new music tonight.

I am still deciding on what the future of the site is going to be like, I might make several parts go Wiki-based, which might stunt development.

Sean Patrick Myrick on September 28th 2019

SNU developer log day 242

I am starting to reach the limits of the functionality of the alpha part of this site. I am also becoming extremely unmotivated to work. There are many issues I am going through, such as low disk space, poor time management, procrastination, and more.

I want to keep developing this draft until it is to the point where I want it before I move on to beta. I just can't keep doing this by myself.

I think tomorrow will be a better day with development.

Sean Patrick Myrick on September 29th 2019
